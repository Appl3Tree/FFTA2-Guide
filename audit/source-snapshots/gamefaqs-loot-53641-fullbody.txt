Section C) How To Find Loot [howto]
~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~+~

Finding loot to steal is rather simple. You need the following to steal loot
effectively:

--a Scholar (Nu Mou, requires 1 Sage ability and 1 Time Mage ability, as well
  as the quest "Treasured Tomes") OR the character Al-Cid
--a Thief with all loot-stealing skills OR a Bishop (Bangaa, requires two White
  Monk abilities) with the Pilfer spell
--someone who can inflict Stone or Stop (Bishops can cause Stone with Break)

Then, just get into a battle and find an enemy. Then, use your Scholar's Study
or Al-Cid's Interrogate to see what loot the foe is holding. Then use the
corresponding loot skill with your Thief, or Pilfer with your Bishop if you
don't want to lose accuracy with each subsequent hit. Still, causing Stop or
Stone will up your chances dramatically.

And the best part is, loot for each mission stays static (except perhaps the
ones with constant reinforcements), so if it's a repeatable mission, you can
go back and get the same loot anytime you want. It can really help you get the
things you want from the Bazaar.

Sub-Section I) Non-Repeatable Missions [norep]

Here, I'll have all of the non-repeatable quests listed by quest line. A note
to consider, though: the quests "The Bangaa of the Rupies" and "The Nu Mou of
the Rupies" will be located here, since they become unavailable after you do
"The Rivalry of the Rupies".

Also, for several of the story missions, it is impossible to have a Scholar by
that point in time, so Astrohawke18 of the FFTA2 boards took the liberty of
hacking his starting Nu Mou into a Scholar and getting this info himself. I've
just taken the liberty of reformatting it to display the same as the stuff I
collected.

Q) Is there any missable loot?

A) The only loot piece you can miss in this game is Flutegrass, during the
  Making Music quest. Also note that you can only get one Strawood.
